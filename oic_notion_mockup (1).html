🔵 Transform Our Health and Homelessness System

Breaking the Cycle (Homelessness) 3 problem areas · 6 issues ▶
Structural / Systemic Issues 3 issues ▶
Issue
Type
Description
Evidence
Housing Unaffordability and Supply Shortage
Regulation & Policy
SF has one of the world's most expensive housing markets. Affordable supply far short of demand.
Median rent $3,148/mo — 55% above US avg. Median homeless income before losing housing: $960/mo.
Why it's a problem
When housing costs vastly exceed incomes, any life disruption — job loss, medical emergency, relationship breakdown — can push people into homelessness. Without affordable alternatives, displaced residents have nowhere to go.
Evidence
Median home price ~$1.29M (2025). Permitting averaged 450+ calendar days — longest of any large US city. 59% of homeless last lived in SF — meaning they were locals, not migrants.
Racial Inequality and Systemic Disparities
Regulation & Policy
Homelessness is deeply racialized. Black, Hispanic/Latine, and TGNC individuals dramatically overrepresented.
Black = ~5% population but 25–37% of homeless. Hispanic/Latine = 15% pop, 34% homeless.
Why it's a problem
Communities historically excluded from homeownership face far greater vulnerability. Without addressing racial equity, homelessness interventions will continue to fail the most marginalized groups.
Poverty, Income Inequality, and Cost of Living
Regulation & Policy
Tech-sector wealth coexists with deep poverty. Housing market structurally inaccessible to low-income residents.
Housing costs in SF are 242% higher than the U.S. average. Homeless income $960/mo vs $3,148 avg rent.

Substance Use and the Fentanyl/Drug Crisis
Resident Behaviour

Public Drug Use & Addiction Crisis 3 issues ▶
Issue
Type
Description
Evidence
Fentanyl Overdose Epidemic
Resident Behaviour
Fentanyl ~50× more potent than heroin. Flooded drug supply since ~2019. Overdose likely even for experienced users.
811 deaths in 2023 (record). 635 in 2024 (−20%). >80% involved fentanyl. Avg 2+ deaths/day at peak.
Why it's a problem
Fentanyl's extreme potency means a microscopic dosing error is fatal. Deaths cluster in open-use areas like the Tenderloin, traumatizing residents, overwhelming EMS, and occurring in public view — including near schools, libraries, and transit stations.
Insufficient Addiction Treatment Capacity
Municipal Process
People with severe SUD far exceed available treatment resources. Many who want help can't access it.
Only 46 city-run treatment spaces at peak crisis. 28% of homeless drug users wanted treatment but couldn't access it.
Drug-Related Violence & Assault
Resident Behaviour
Drug markets associated with firearms, turf conflicts, armed robbery, dealer-on-dealer violence.
Gun violence in Tenderloin +77%. 1,013 drug offense incidents in 12-month period ending Aug 2023.
